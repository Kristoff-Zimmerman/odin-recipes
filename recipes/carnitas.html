Carnitas

Carnitas, literally meaning "little meats", is a dish of Mexican cuisine that originated in the state of Michoac√°n. Carnitas can be used as an ingredient in tamales, tacos, tortas, and burritos.

Ingredients

  • 4 pounds skinless, boneless pork butt or shoulder
  • 3-4 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 large brown or white onion, cut into wedges
  • 8 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 limes, juiced
  • 2 large oranges, juiced
  • 3/4 cup Mexican Coca-Cola
  • 2 bay leaves

Instructions

  1. Rinse and pat dry pork with a paper towel
  2. Add pork, salt, pepper, oregano, cumin, onion, garlic, lime juice, orange juice, coke, and bay leaves to a slow cooker
  3. Cover and cook on low heat for 8-10 hours, or hight heat for 5-6 hours
  4. Remove pork and shred (Do not discard the liquid)
  5. Heat oil in skillet over high heat
  6. In batches sear pork with a ladle full of liquid from the slow cooker
  7. Contiue cooding until the juices begin to reduce and pork is crispy
  8. Serve with extra salt, pepper, or slowe cooker liquid if desired.
